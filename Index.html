1 BRS Structure & Mission

Video: https://youtu.be/WQ2huYtdZWU

An overview of how Breeding and Research Services (BRS) is organized: Trial & Nursery, Lab Services, and Breeding Analytics are designed to work as one pipeline. The goal is to deliver consistent, high-quality data and operational support from field trial design to sample handling to downstream genetic and phenotypic analysis — with shared standards, shared tools, and shared service delivery. 2 Access to Genotyping (DArT Services)

Video: https://youtu.be/jBaJ0_Ug_Zs

NARES partners can access DArT genotyping through BRS at negotiated, discounted rates. Instead of every program negotiating alone, Lab Services centralizes that access through a shared services model, so national programs can submit samples and receive high-quality marker data without having to build full in-house genotyping capacity. [oai_citation:5‡Lab Services Overview and instructions.pdf](sediment://file_00000000c07461f5982ccd3bc0a0ea75)

3 Barcoding & Sample Traceability

Video: https://youtu.be/uCvoxYF6IlE

Every sample must be labeled clearly and consistently. Lab Services uses barcodes generated in EBS that encode lab ID, batch number, and sample ID. This prevents mislabeling, mix-ups between plates, and data loss later. The barcode is the anchor that ties physical material to digital records, so downstream analytics and reports are trusted. [oai_citation:6‡Lab Services Overview and instructions.pdf](sediment://file_00000000c07461f5982ccd3bc0a0ea75)

4 Leaf Sampling & Plate Setup

Video: https://youtu.be/fefiCpUGidI

This module covers how to collect tissue for genotyping: how many leaf discs to take, how to select healthy material, how to avoid contamination, how to lay samples into 96-well plates or punch mats, and how to record plate layouts for tracking. It links field practice directly to lab data quality. [oai_citation:7‡Lab Services Overview and instructions.pdf](sediment://file_00000000c07461f5982ccd3bc0a0ea75)

5 Field Collection Kits (Envelopes, Cluster Tubes)

Videos: https://youtu.be/sfKlxMbxMAc  |  https://youtu.be/NI1S_apxxk8

Proper sample kits matter. Pre-labeled envelopes and cluster tubes speed up punching, keep samples organized by plate layout, and reduce transcription errors. The guidance stresses: pick healthy tissue, avoid contamination, record metadata, and document everything on standardized QA/QC forms so nothing is lost between field and lab. [oai_citation:8‡Lab Services Overview and instructions.pdf](sediment://file_00000000c07461f5982ccd3bc0a0ea75)

6 Drying, Packing & International Shipping

Videos: https://youtu.be/rsd7ZueBNc4  |  https://youtu.be/st1_9dk8Kzk

Freeze-drying (vs. oven drying) is used to stabilize tissue for downstream analysis. Staff log sample handling in lab books, label all material with barcodes, and package it for export. For cross-border shipments, teams must prepare invoices, phytosanitary certificates, import permits, and “research use only” language so materials clear customs without delay. [oai_citation:9‡Lab Services Overview and instructions.pdf](sediment://file_00000000c07461f5982ccd3bc0a0ea75)

7 Elemental Analysis & Nutrition Profiling

Video: https://youtu.be/3_BtxbxeGM0

High-throughput elemental analysis (for traits like iron, zinc, and provitamin A) is now routine in biofortification breeding. The team uses X-ray fluorescence (XRF), adapted from mining, as a fast, non-destructive, cost-effective alternative to slower reference chemistry. Strategic labs in places like Rwanda, India, and Bangladesh are processing tens of thousands of samples per year, giving breeders nutrition data at the speed of breeding. [oai_citation:10‡Lab Services Overview and instructions.pdf](sediment://file_00000000c07461f5982ccd3bc0a0ea75)

8 Sample Preparation for Quality Testing

Video: https://youtu.be/k6kOlqSi1oY

This module sets out practical rules for collecting grain / tissue for downstream quality analysis. Topics include: getting a truly representative sample, preventing contamination, drying to the right moisture content, polishing rice to realistic levels, sizing samples correctly (e.g. minimum grams needed), and preparing documents for international shipment. Following these steps protects data quality and keeps shipments compliant. [oai_citation:11‡Lab Services Overview and instructions.pdf](sediment://file_00000000c07461f5982ccd3bc0a0ea75)

9 From Service Request to Delivery

Video: https://youtu.be/CH84-U_3JxM

Lab Services is standardizing the full pipeline from intake to delivery using Business Process Management and Quality Management System practices. Processes are being mapped end-to-end (including roles and responsibilities), SOPs are being locked in, and data formats are being harmonized so that analytics workflows are consistent across crops, regions, and vendors. The goal is a single CGIAR service portal — one front door for genotyping, elemental analysis, and more — with faster turnaround and traceable, auditable data.
